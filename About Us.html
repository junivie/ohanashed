Appointment Policy


The OHANA SHED clinic highly encourage the Pet Owners to book first an appointment before visiting the clinic to have a secured slot for consultation and visit.

1. Appointment should be booked before visiting to clinic, except for emergency care patients.
2. All pets are required to be in leash or placed on pet carriers before entering the clinic to avoid any clash between animals in waiting area.
3. Waiting room for Boarding pets are different from pets with their owners around. Boarding pets do not necessarily go to clinic for consultation, but can be advantage for pet owners who do not have the ample of time for a fixed scheduled consultation. Consultation for boarding pets depends on vacant and availability of the vet doctors.


Emergency Care Policy


During Business Hours:
Contact the primary veterinary clinic number: [Your Clinic Phone Number]
Inform the receptionist about the nature of the emergency.

After Business Hours:
Call our dedicated emergency line: [Emergency Contact Number]
Clearly state your name, contact number, and details of the emergency.
Follow the instructions provided by the emergency line operator.

Emergency Care Procedures:

Assessment:
Upon arrival, our veterinary team will conduct a quick assessment of the animal's condition.
Immediate stabilization measures will be initiated, if necessary.

Notification:
The pet owner will be contacted as soon as possible.
In case of unavailability, the emergency contact person listed in the pet's records will be notified.

Authorization:
For any necessary emergency procedures, our veterinary team will discuss treatment options and associated costs with the pet owner or the designated emergency contact person.
Emergency treatment will be initiated upon receiving authorization.

Records and Documentation:
Comprehensive records of the emergency assessment, treatment, and procedures performed will be documented in the patient's medical history.

Transfer to Specialized Facilities:
In cases requiring specialized care beyond the clinic's capabilities, the veterinary team will facilitate the transfer to an appropriate emergency or specialty care facility.

Client Education:
Pet owners will be provided with information on recognizing potential emergency situations and encouraged to keep an updated list of emergency contacts.

Review and Training:
This Emergency Care Policy will be regularly reviewed and updated to reflect the latest standards and best practices. All staff members will undergo periodic training on emergency procedures.

Acknowledgment:
Pet owners are encouraged to acknowledge and understand the Emergency Care Policy. Any questions or concerns can be addressed with our veterinary team during regular business hours.

Privacy Notice


Personal Information
1. We collect general information about the pet owners such as Full Name and Contact Information for easy identification of pet patients and faster reaching out to its owners.
2. Pet Medical History - To allow vets identify the health status of the pet and will be used as reference during consultation.

Information We Keep
1. Personal Information and Pet medical History will remain confidential and will be kept by the clinic as long as the Owners visit the clinic.
2. Pet Medical History will remain on the clinic's system and will not be given to other clinic except when the owner requested the transfer of medical history.
3. Both Information will be kept in the clinic's system as long as the owners are still customer of the clinic. Terminating of services also means disposal of the personal informations unless it's required to be kept for legal purposes.
